## The Attention Economy Model

**Attention Economy (in this context):** The observable phenomenon where finite media bandwidth is consumed by high-visibility performative events, creating predictable information deficits in other domains.

**Quotas (in this context):** The minimum threshold of media attention consumption that each high-visibility official is expected to generate. When an official falls below quota or generates counterproductive attention, they are replaced.

Rather than suggesting coordination, the model posits that:
1. **Attention is finite** — news cycles have limited bandwidth
2. **Performative officials fill quotas** — each generates a predictable volume of controversy, consuming measurable media cycles
3. **When one performer underperforms, they are replaced** — the system requires consistent noise generation
4. **This pattern is consistent across both Trump administrations** (2017–2021 and 2025–present)

## What This Analysis Claims

1. High-saturation performers consume **predictable** media bandwidth
2. Performative peaks **correlate** with institutional transition windows
3. Each individual occupies a **pressure point** relevant to document control, enforcement messaging, or budget authority
4. Exit timing **matters** (Bongino's departure around Dec 19 deadline)
5. **Replacement escalation is consistent across both administrations** — underperformers are replaced by louder successors
6. The pattern is **structurally self-regulating** — officials who generate counterproductive attention are cycled out

## What This Analysis Does NOT Claim

- ❌ No claim of conscious coordination — the pattern could be emergent
- ❌ No claim of central direction — Trump's management style may naturally select for high-noise officials
- ❌ No claim that performativity *causes* institutional events
- ❌ No claim about individual motivations or intent
- ❌ No claim that underperforming events are staged — the Minneapolis killings were real events; the "failure" was in Noem's inability to manage the aftermath productively

### Testable Predictions

- When a high-visibility official is removed, a replacement will be selected who generates *equal or greater* media volume
- Compliance events will cluster more heavily during replacement transition windows
- The Mullin confirmation process will generate substantial media coverage, during which institutional events will proceed with reduced scrutiny

## The "Epstein Class" Counter-Frame (March 2026)

**Key Finding**: [Inference] The friction mechanism appears to have partially escaped the thermostat's control. Instead of attention being successfully captured by the Iran war, a bipartisan counter-label has emerged that persistently links the war back to the Epstein files. This suggests the thermostat model has limits — when friction reaches a certain intensity, the distraction itself may become evidence of the thing being distracted from.

### The Mechanism

The "Epstein class" label functions as an attention-economy short-circuit: rather than the kinetic event (Iran strikes) consuming all available bandwidth and displacing Epstein coverage, critics across party lines reframe the kinetic event itself as proof of the leverage thesis. This creates a feedback loop where every escalation in the war reinforces the Epstein narrative rather than suppressing it.

### Framework Implications

[Inference] This counter-frame suggests a potential thermostat failure mode: the distraction mechanism may become self-defeating when friction intensity is high enough. Key observations:

1. **The label persists**: Unlike typical news cycles where attention shifts cleanly, the "Epstein class" label embeds the Epstein connection into the war coverage itself
2. **Bipartisan uptake**: Both Republican and Democratic figures independently arrived at the same framing, suggesting it resonates across the political spectrum
3. **Feedback loop**: Each escalation in Iran creates new opportunities to invoke the "Epstein class" frame, making the distraction counterproductive
4. **Testable prediction**: If the thermostat model is correct, the intensity of the counter-frame should correlate with friction escalation — more war coverage should produce more "Epstein class" invocations, not fewer
