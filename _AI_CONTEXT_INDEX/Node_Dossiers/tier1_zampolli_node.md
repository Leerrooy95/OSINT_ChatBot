### Mechanism: Diplomatic Title as Multi-Jurisdictional Legal Shield

The April 11, 2026 Friction Breaker output detected a new mechanism it provisionally named "Diplomatic Title as Legal Shield (Special Envoy Immunization)" with a durability rating of 4/10. Real-world execution since April 11 indicates the mechanism is structurally more durable than initially scored:

| Component | Observed Behavior |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Diplomatic-title legitimacy** | The "Special Envoy of the President" title in Zampolli's X bio and legal filings creates a presumption of credibility that ordinary plaintiffs do not have, making press counsel more cautious about pre-publication review |
| **Multi-jurisdictional filing** | Italy, Brazil, and US filings simultaneously force respondents to defend in three legal systems with different evidentiary standards, anti-SLAPP protections, and discovery rules. Italy lacks robust anti-SLAPP protections; Brazil has limited press-protection precedent for foreign-national plaintiffs; the US has Section 230 protections for platforms but not for individual amplifiers |
| **Class-action framing** | The "any media outlet or individual that publishes, supports, or amplifies" framing is legally weak (defamation is fact-specific and cannot be class-actioned in this manner under most Western law), but produces a chilling effect on amplification regardless of legal viability |
| **"AI surveillance" framing** | Statement that legal team is using "advanced artificial intelligence" to identify critics on social media and podcasts creates personal-targeting fear among low-resource creators (independent journalists, podcasters, OSINT researchers) who cannot afford defense costs |
| **Diplomatic immunity uncertainty** | Special Envoy titles do not confer diplomatic immunity in standard usage, but the title's existence creates ambiguity that benefits Zampolli in pre-litigation negotiation |

[Inference] The mechanism's actual durability is closer to 6-7/10 in execution, not 4/10. It is operating at scale specifically because the targets (Ungaro in Brazil with limited resources, RAI's *Report* facing an Italian prosecutor's complaint, US media facing a presidential-envoy plaintiff) have asymmetric defensive capacity. The mechanism does not require Zampolli to win — it requires defendants to incur defense costs.
