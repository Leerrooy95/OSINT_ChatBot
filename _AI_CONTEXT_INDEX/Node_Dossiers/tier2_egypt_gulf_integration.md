## 2013 Post-Coup Financial Architecture

### Gulf Bailout of Sisi Government

Following the July 2013 military coup that removed President Mohamed Morsi, three Gulf states provided immediate financial support to the new military-backed government under General Abdel Fattah el-Sisi.

| Country | Total Aid (2013-2014) | Components | Verification |
|---------|----------------------|------------|--------------|
| Saudi Arabia | $5 billion | $1B grant, $2B central bank deposit, $2B petroleum products | ✅ VERIFIED — [Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/menasource/what-will-the-gulfs-12-billion-buy-in-egypt/), [France 24](https://www.france24.com/en/20130709-saudi-arabia-uae-approve-billions-aid-egypt) |
| UAE | $3 billion | $1B grant, $2B interest-free central bank deposit | ✅ VERIFIED — [France 24](https://www.france24.com/en/20130709-saudi-arabia-uae-approve-billions-aid-egypt) |
| Kuwait | $4 billion | $1B grant, $2B central bank deposit, $1B oil products | ✅ VERIFIED — [Daily News Egypt](https://www.dailynewsegypt.com/2013/07/10/egypt-to-receive-4bn-in-aid-from-kuwait/) |
| **TOTAL** | **$12 billion+** | Mix of deposits, grants, fuel shipments | ✅ VERIFIED — [Chatham House](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2020/04/egypt-and-gulf/sisis-debt-his-gulf-arab-backers), [Washington Institute](https://www.washingtoninstitute.org/policy-analysis/aid-security-gulf-egypt-dynamic-supporting-egypts-economy) |

**Context**: Egypt's foreign reserves were near critical lows following political instability. Gulf support totaled $12-23B over the first 18 months, depending on accounting methodology. Total Gulf support for Egypt from 2011-2022 estimated at over $92B.

**Source**: [Chatham House — Sisi's Debt to His Gulf Arab Backers](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2020/04/egypt-and-gulf/sisis-debt-his-gulf-arab-backers)
