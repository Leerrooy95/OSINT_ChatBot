## The Structural Function

**Thesis**: High-visibility controversies ("flashbangs") consume media attention during windows when institutional friction events occur. The "main characters" generate noise that makes pattern detection possible.

**Key Insight**: When tracking what moves *during* peak saturation windows, correlations emerge between:
- Document release schedules
- Institutional compliance events
- Capital movements and diplomatic shifts
- The 7-day median lagged response patterns

## What This Analysis Claims

1. High-saturation performers consume **predictable** media bandwidth
2. Performative peaks **correlate** with institutional transition windows
3. Each individual occupies a **pressure point** relevant to document control, enforcement messaging, or budget authority
4. Exit timing **matters** (Bongino's departure around Dec 19 deadline)

## What This Analysis Does NOT Claim

- No claim that individuals consciously coordinate
- No claim that performativity *causes* institutional events
- No claim about individual motivations or intent

**The claim is structural**: The "main characters" generate predictable noise patterns that enable detection of quieter correlations.
